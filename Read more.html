Longer version


D-Art aims to solve the trouble of the affordability of equipment which leads to the hesitancy of pursuing the Creative Economy. Budget has become a problem when purchasing equipment to pursue the Creative Economy as a career choice as this equipment can be expensive to many. Among the top inquiries of Art students would be affordable equipment and would go through lengths going through researching in different links and videos that would help them decide, but D-Art can make it easier with having it all in one website with all the needed information. Our Company can provide experienced reviews from people who have handled certain equipment which are alternatives and cheaper than other products. Recommended brands and equipment are posted and up to date whether you need gadgets, software, and arts and crafts. Links for numerous online stores will be listed and news for sales and discounts will be posted and notified. Not only would we provide the links for official stores, but we would have links for secondhand equipment for a much more affordable price compared to the original.

Having the best equipment can be an investment in pursuing a career, however the problem lies whether one can buy it or not. The fear is present when people think of cheaper equipment because commonly it would mean the quality of it is half-baked or problematic. The company wishes to solve this problem by already having a list of recommendations on gadgets with reviews of the equipment in its own website page. The reviews we give for each product would include what to expect in the box, Pros and Cons in a descriptive and more detailed manner, a summary of the pros and cons, the product to price ratio to see itâ€™s worth, and additional information of the equipment. There will not be any ratings as it would hinder the psychological thought process when choosing an equipment with the mentality that a higher rating is better and needed.


We understand that artists have different needs and have different standards when choosing their equipment/materials. We do not advertise the products that would be shown on the website and to try of persuading the buyer to purchase a specific equipment. The website serves as a convenient guide to find what equipment the artist/buyer is looking for.
